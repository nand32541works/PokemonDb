POKÉMON TYPES

Pokémon Types are a fundamental aspect of battle strategy and world-building within the Pokémon universe. Every Pokémon species is assigned one or two types, such as Fire, Water, Grass, Electric, or Dragon. These types influence not only a Pokémon's strengths and weaknesses in battle but also determine move effectiveness, resistances, and immunities. For example, Water-type moves are super effective against Fire-types but weak against Grass-types.


There are currently 18 different Pokémon types, ranging from classics like Normal, Psychic, and Fighting, to unique types like Fairy and Steel, which were introduced in later generations to balance battle mechanics. Some Pokémon, known as dual-types, possess two different types, providing them with a diverse range of move advantages and resistances.


Mastering type matchups is crucial for trainers seeking success in battles, gym challenges, and tournaments. Knowing which type combinations to use against opponents can turn the tide of battle, while poor type choices can lead to defeat. Additionally, some moves have unique effects depending on type, such as Electric moves being ineffective against Ground-types or Steel-types having high resistance to most attacks.

TYPE MATCHUPS AND STRATEGY

Understanding type matchups is the key to mastering Pokémon battles. Each type has its own offensive and defensive advantages and disadvantages. For example, Electric-type moves are powerful against Water and Flying-types but completely ineffective against Ground-types. Meanwhile, Fairy-types were introduced in Generation VI to balance powerful types like Dragon.


Trainers often build teams by combining a variety of types to cover weaknesses and exploit opponents' vulnerabilities. Dual-type Pokémon, such as Charizard (Fire/Flying) or Garchomp (Dragon/Ground), can create unique strategic advantages in battles, though they may also inherit multiple weaknesses.


The Pokémon Database offers an interactive type matchup chart and guides to help trainers choose the best type combinations and understand how move effectiveness works. This ensures trainers can enter battles with confidence and a solid tactical plan.

DUAL-TYPE POKÉMON

Many Pokémon possess two types, known as dual-types, which grant them a broader range of offensive and defensive options. While this can create interesting advantages, such as gaining extra resistances or neutralizing weaknesses, it can also expose them to additional vulnerabilities.


Dual-types like Tyranitar (Rock/Dark), Lucario (Fighting/Steel), and Gyarados (Water/Flying) are popular among competitive trainers for their unique battle versatility. Understanding how type interactions multiply or cancel out is essential when facing dual-type opponents in battles.


Other some examples of Dual-Type Pokémon:

Water/Ground: Quagsire, Marshtomp, Seismitoad, Swampert, etc.

Grass/Dark: Shiftry, Cacturne, Meowscarada, etc.

Flying/Dragon: Dragonite, Altaria, Salamence, etc.

Normal/Flying: Taillow, Pidgeot, Staraptor, etc.

Psychic/Fairy: Kirlia, Mr. Mime, Tapu-lele, etc.

Rock/Steal: Aggron, Shieldon, Probopass, etc.

POKÉMON TYPES & DETAILS

There are 18 official Pokémon types, each representing elemental properties and battle characteristics. Each type affects battle strengths, weaknesses, move effectiveness, and resistances. Here’s a breakdown of each type and what makes it unique:

Normal Type

Normal-type Pokémon are known for their simplicity and balanced stats. They don’t have many strengths but only one weakness: Fighting-type moves. Their moves typically lack elemental effects but offer reliable damage.

Fire Type

Fire-type Pokémon are fierce and passionate, known for powerful flame-based attacks. They’re strong against Grass, Bug, Ice, and Steel types but weak to Water, Ground, and Rock moves.

Water Type

Water-types are abundant and versatile, capable of learning a variety of offensive and defensive moves. They’re strong against Fire, Ground, and Rock types but weak to Electric and Grass moves.

Grass Type

Grass-type Pokémon draw power from nature and are known for moves that drain health, inflict status conditions, or heal allies. They’re effective against Water, Rock, and Ground types but weak to Fire, Ice, Poison, Bug, and Flying.

Electric Type

Electric-types use high-voltage attacks and are known for their speed and special attack stats. They’re effective against Water and Flying types but weak to Ground moves, which are immune to Electric attacks.

Ice Type

Ice-types harness the cold to freeze opponents, slowing them down or rendering them immobile. They’re strong against Dragon, Grass, Ground, and Flying types but weak to Fire, Rock, Fighting, and Steel moves.

Fighting Type

Fighting-types excel in physical combat and possess high attack stats. They’re strong against Normal, Rock, Steel, Ice, and Dark types, but vulnerable to Psychic, Flying, and Fairy moves.

Poison Type

Poison-types are known for inflicting status conditions like poison and toxic damage. They’re effective against Grass and Fairy types but weak to Ground and Psychic moves.

Ground Type

Ground-types control the earth and excel against Electric, Fire, Poison, Rock, and Steel types. They’re immune to Electric moves but weak to Water, Ice, and Grass attacks.

Flying Type

Flying-types are agile and often paired with other types. They’re effective against Grass, Bug, and Fighting types but weak to Electric, Rock, and Ice moves.

Psychic Type

Psychic-types possess powerful mental abilities and high special attack stats. They’re strong against Fighting and Poison types but weak to Bug, Ghost, and Dark moves.

Bug Type

Bug-types are often fast and evolve early. They’re effective against Grass, Psychic, and Dark types but vulnerable to Fire, Flying, and Rock attacks.

Rock Type

Rock-types are tough defenders with strong resistance to Normal, Flying, Fire, and Poison moves. They’re effective against Flying, Bug, Fire, and Ice types but weak to Water, Grass, Ground, Steel, and Fighting moves.

Ghost Type

Ghost-types are mysterious and immune to Normal and Fighting moves. They’re strong against Psychic and Ghost types but weak to Dark moves.

Dragon Type

Dragon-types are rare, powerful, and boast high stats. They’re strong against other Dragon types but weak to Ice, Fairy, and other Dragon moves.

Dark Type

Dark-types specialize in underhanded tactics like flinching and status moves. They’re strong against Psychic and Ghost types but weak to Fairy, Bug, and Fighting moves.

Steel Type

Steel-types have incredible defenses and resist many move types. They’re strong against Ice, Rock, and Fairy types but weak to Fire, Fighting, and Ground moves.

Fairy Type

Fairy-types were introduced to counter Dragon-types. They’re effective against Dragon, Dark, and Fighting types, while weak to Steel and Poison moves.
